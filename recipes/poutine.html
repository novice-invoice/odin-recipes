Poutine

Description

Poutine is a popular Canadian dish that originated in Quebec. It consists of crispy French fries that are topped with fresh cheese curds and rich gravy. The dish is typically made by first frying the French fries until they are golden and crispy, then adding a generous amount of cheese curds on top. Finally, a hot gravy made from beef or chicken stock is poured over the fries and cheese curds, creating a delicious and savory flavor combination. Poutine can be served as a hearty snack or as a main dish, and is often enjoyed with a cold beer or other beverage. It has become a beloved staple of Canadian cuisine and can now be found in many restaurants and food trucks throughout the country.

Ingredients

  • 4-5 large potatoes, peeled and cut into thin fries
  • 2 cups of beef or chicken stock
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cheese curds
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

  2. Rinse the cut fries in cold water to remove excess starch. Dry them with a clean kitchen towel.
  3. Spread the fries on a baking sheet in a single layer. Drizzle with oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Bake for 20-30 minutes until golden and crispy.
  4. While the fries are baking, make the gravy. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk until smooth. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  5. Gradually add the beef or chicken stock, whisking constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 5-7 minutes until thickened.
  6. Place the fries in a serving bowl. Sprinkle the cheese curds on top.
  7. Pour the hot gravy over the fries and cheese curds, making sure everything is covered.
